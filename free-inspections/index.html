Why Get a Roof Inspection?

Your roof takes a beating every single day — sun, rain, snow, ice, wind, and temperature swings. Most roof problems start small and invisible, but left unchecked, they turn into expensive emergencies. A simple inspection catches issues early, when they're cheap and easy to fix.

Whether you've noticed something concerning, your roof is aging, a storm just passed through, or you're buying/selling a home — a professional inspection gives you clarity and peace of mind.

When You Should Get an Inspection

✓

After a Major Storm

Hail, high winds, and falling debris can damage your roof in ways invisible from the ground. Get an inspection after every significant weather event.

✓

Your Roof Is 15+ Years Old

Most asphalt shingle roofs last 20-30 years. If yours is past the halfway point, an inspection helps you plan ahead instead of reacting to an emergency.

✓

You See Warning Signs

Water stains on ceilings, granules in gutters, curling shingles, sagging areas, or daylight in the attic — any of these warrant an immediate inspection.

✓

Buying or Selling a Home

A roof inspection report gives buyers confidence and gives sellers documentation that their roof is in good shape. It's a smart move on both sides.

What We Inspect

  • Shingle condition — curling, cracking, missing, granule loss
  • Flashing around chimneys, vents, skylights, and walls
  • Vent pipe boots and roof penetrations
  • Ridge caps and hip shingles
  • Gutters, drip edge, and fascia boards
  • Soffit and attic ventilation
  • Signs of water damage, rot, or mold
  • Overall structural integrity

What Happens After the Inspection

We'll share our findings with you in plain English — no jargon, no scare tactics. If your roof is in good shape, we'll tell you. If there are issues, we'll explain your options, give you a written estimate if any work is needed, and let you decide on your own time. Zero pressure, ever.
